ChemE Library

The department's centralized academic repository for Chemical Engineering students.

The ChemE Library is the department's centralized academic repository, created to give every Chemical Engineering student structured and dependable access to essential course resources. Organized semester-wise, it covers all core subjects and brings together lecture notes, tutorials, quizzes, minors, majors, solution sets, and curated topper handouts — all systematically indexed in one place.

Designed to eliminate scattered materials and last-minute searching, the library provides a streamlined academic framework that supports both exam preparation and deep conceptual learning. The content evolves alongside course updates, ensuring students always have access to relevant and well-organized material.

More than a collection of files, the ChemE Library represents a culture of academic continuity and collaboration — built by students, strengthened across batches, and maintained to support consistent excellence within the department.

Library Features

  • Semester-wise organization
  • All core subjects covered
  • Lecture notes & tutorials
  • Quiz papers & solutions
  • Topper handouts
  • Regularly updated content

Career Guidance

Comprehensive roadmaps and requirements for various career paths available to Chemical Engineering graduates.

Software Development Engineer (SDE)

Design, build, test, and maintain software applications and complex systems. SDEs are the backbone of tech companies, working on everything from user interfaces to backend infrastructure.

Roadmap & Requirements:

  • Programming: Master one language (C++, Java, or Python)
  • Data Structures & Algorithms: Practice extensively on LeetCode, Codeforces
  • Development Projects: Learn a tech stack (MERN, Spring Boot, mobile app dev) and build 2-3 substantial projects
  • CS Fundamentals: OOP, OS, DBMS, Computer Networks
  • System Design: Basics of high-level and low-level system design

Data Analytics / Data Science

Analyze large sets of raw data to uncover trends, extract actionable insights, and help businesses make data-driven decisions.

Roadmap & Requirements:

  • Database & Querying: Master SQL (Joins, Window Functions, Subqueries)
  • Programming: Learn Python or R, focusing on data manipulation (Pandas, NumPy)
  • Data Visualization: Tableau, PowerBI, or Excel proficiency
  • Math & Stats: Probability, descriptive/inferential statistics, hypothesis testing
  • Machine Learning: Foundational algorithms (Linear/Logistic Regression, Decision Trees)
  • Business Acumen: Understanding of business applications

Quantitative Analyst / Researcher (Quant)

Apply rigorous mathematical and statistical models to financial markets. They build algorithms to identify trading opportunities and manage risk.

Roadmap & Requirements:

  • Advanced Mathematics: Calculus, Linear Algebra, Probability, Stochastic Calculus
  • Brainteasers & Logic: "Heard on the Street" or "Fifty Challenging Problems in Probability"
  • Programming: Master C++ (for high-frequency trading) or Python (for research)
  • Finance Basics: Financial markets, derivatives, trading strategies
  • Background: Competitive programming (ICPC) or Math Olympiads experience

Machine Learning (ML) Engineer

Bridge the gap between data science and software engineering. They take theoretical ML models and write the code to deploy them at scale in production environments.

Roadmap & Requirements:

  • Foundation: Python, Pandas, NumPy, Matplotlib, DSA, Linear Algebra, Calculus, Probability
  • ML Algorithms: Classical ML using Scikit-Learn
  • Deep Learning: PyTorch or TensorFlow (Neural Networks, NLP, Computer Vision)
  • MLOps & Deployment: Docker, FastAPI/Flask, AWS/GCP
  • Projects: End-to-end ML projects with deployment

Management / Strategy Consulting

Advise companies on high-level strategic decisions, operational improvements, market entry, and profitability issues.

Roadmap & Requirements:

  • Profile Building: Strong academics, PORs, extracurricular achievements
  • Guesstimates: Market-sizing questions and estimation skills
  • Case Studies: Frameworks for Profitability, Market Entry, Pricing, M&A (Case in Point, Victor Cheng)
  • Soft Skills: Communication, presentation, behavioral interview skills
  • Business Skills: Strategic thinking without complete information

Core Engineering

Apply fundamental engineering physics and math to design, analyze, test, and manufacture physical systems, hardware, or chemical processes.

Roadmap & Requirements:

  • Academic Mastery: Strong fundamentals in core curriculum
  • Software & Tooling: Industry-standard software (Aspen, COMSOL, ANSYS, MATLAB)
  • Hands-on Projects: Research assistance and practical projects
  • Aptitude: Numerical, verbal, and logical reasoning tests
  • Modern Skills: ML and Data Analytics for industry trends

Roadmap

1. Network with Seniors

Identify seniors with placements/interns in target domains and reach out to them:

  • Consulting
  • Core Engineering
  • Tech/Software
  • Data Analytics

2. Preparation Questions

Ask seniors about their experiences:

  • The Filter: "What was the format of the Online Test? Technical, aptitude, or mixed?"
  • Group Discussion: "If there was a GD, what was the topic? Abstract or industry-related?"
  • Round Breakdown: "How many rounds and what was the focus of each?"
  • Preparation Timeline: "When did you start preparing and how did you balance academics?"
  • Key Resources: "What books, websites, or YouTube channels were crucial?"
  • Common Mistakes: "What would you do differently?"
